prime_multiset :
Type

The type of multisets of prime numbers. Unique factorization gives an equivalence between this set and ℕ+, as we will formalize below.

We can forget the primality property and regard a multiset of primes as just a multiset of positive integers, or a multiset of natural numbers. In the opposite direction, if we have a multiset of positive integers or natural numbers, together with a proof that all the elements are prime, then we can regard it as a multiset of primes. The next block of results records obvious properties of these coercions.

prime_multiset.prod_zero :
prime_multiset.prod 0 = 1

The product map gives a homomorphism from the additive monoid of multisets to the multiplicative monoid ℕ+.

prime_multiset.prod_add (u v : prime_multiset) :
prime_multiset.prod (u + v) = prime_multiset.prod u * prime_multiset.prod v

prime_multiset.prod_smul (d : ℕ) (u : prime_multiset) :
prime_multiset.prod (add_monoid.smul d u) = prime_multiset.prod u ^ d

pnat.factor_multiset (n : ℕ+) :
prime_multiset

The prime factors of n, regarded as a multiset

pnat.prod_factor_multiset (n : ℕ+) :
prime_multiset.prod (pnat.factor_multiset n) = n

The product of the factors is the original number

pnat.coe_nat_factor_multiset (n : ℕ+) :
↑(pnat.factor_multiset n) = ↑(nat.factors ↑n)

prime_multiset.factor_multiset_prod (v : prime_multiset) :
pnat.factor_multiset (prime_multiset.prod v) = v

If we start with a multiset of primes, take the product and then factor it, we get back the original multiset.

pnat.factor_multiset_equiv :
ℕ+ ≃ prime_multiset

Positive integers biject with multisets of primes.

pnat.factor_multiset_one :
pnat.factor_multiset 1 = 0

Factoring gives a homomorphism from the multiplicative monoid ℕ+ to the additive monoid of multisets.

pnat.factor_multiset_mul (n m : ℕ+) :
pnat.factor_multiset (n * m) = pnat.factor_multiset n + pnat.factor_multiset m

pnat.factor_multiset_pow (n : ℕ+) (m : ℕ) :
pnat.factor_multiset (n ^ m) = add_monoid.smul m (pnat.factor_multiset n)

pnat.factor_multiset_of_prime (p : nat.primes) :
pnat.factor_multiset ↑p = prime_multiset.of_prime p

Factoring a prime gives the corresponding one-element multiset.

pnat.factor_multiset_le_iff {m n : ℕ+} :
pnat.factor_multiset m ≤ pnat.factor_multiset n ↔ m ∣ n

We now have four different results that all encode the idea that inequality of multisets corresponds to divisibility of positive integers.

pnat.factor_multiset_le_iff' {m : ℕ+} {v : prime_multiset} :
pnat.factor_multiset m ≤ v ↔ m ∣ prime_multiset.prod v

prime_multiset.prod_dvd_iff {u v : prime_multiset} :
prime_multiset.prod u ∣ prime_multiset.prod v ↔ u ≤ v

prime_multiset.prod_dvd_iff' {u : prime_multiset} {n : ℕ+} :
prime_multiset.prod u ∣ n ↔ u ≤ pnat.factor_multiset n

pnat.factor_multiset_gcd (m n : ℕ+) :
pnat.factor_multiset (pnat.gcd m n) = pnat.factor_multiset m ⊓ pnat.factor_multiset n

The gcd and lcm operations on positive integers correspond to the inf and sup operations on multisets.

pnat.factor_multiset_lcm (m n : ℕ+) :
pnat.factor_multiset (pnat.lcm m n) = pnat.factor_multiset m ⊔ pnat.factor_multiset n

pnat.count_factor_multiset (m : ℕ+) (p : nat.primes) (k : ℕ) :
↑p ^ k ∣ m ↔ k ≤ multiset.count p (pnat.factor_multiset m)

The number of occurrences of p in the factor multiset of m is the same as the p-adic valuation of m.

prime_multiset.prod_inf (u v : prime_multiset) :
prime_multiset.prod (u ⊓ v) = pnat.gcd (prime_multiset.prod u) (prime_multiset.prod v)

prime_multiset.prod_sup (u v : prime_multiset) :
prime_multiset.prod (u ⊔ v) = pnat.lcm (prime_multiset.prod u) (prime_multiset.prod v)
